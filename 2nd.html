Healed Through AI

Artificial intelligence was initially developed to replace doctors. Researchers developed robots that could question you, analyze your responses using a learning algorithm, and identify whether you had the flu or a cold. However, because to the insufficient artificial intelligence algorithms used to implement them, these expectations were mostly unmet.

Individual heterogeneity was particularly difficult since it made it impossible for machine learning algorithms to detect trends. However, deep learning, a subfield of AI, ultimately gained the sensitivity to identify speech from voice data. Despite the fact that deep learning algorithms required a large amount of training data, they may eventually learn to recognise words regardless of accents and other variances in speech patterns.

Although object identification in image data is where deep learning is most frequently employed currently, it was initially used to understand speech. To recognize and avoid humans, for instance, driverless cars mainly rely on deep learning to ensure that their passengers get home safely. Deep learning is being used in the medical industry by a number of companies, including the San Francisco- based startup Enlitic, to detect aberrant tumors on radiological pictures that are likely cancerous.
